Police Shooting Data

The interactive map above depicts the locations of police shootings within the past couple of decades. Each circle marker represents a different person and location. Data points when clicked include information on the race of each wounded individual and what type of weapon he/she was wielding if any. (Data from Alaska and Hawaii can be accessed by dragging the map to their locations.)

The colors of each marker represent different United States regions, including the West Coast (yellow), Rockies (purple), Plains (orange), Midwest (blue), Southeast (red), and East Coast (green).

Race/Ethnicity is defined by the outline of each circle marker. The smaller circles represent people of unknown race. The circles with a lighter black outline represent people who are White, and the circles with a darker black outline represent people who are Minorities.

This map was supported by Leaflet.js, OpenStreetMap, and crowdsourced data from Deadspin.
